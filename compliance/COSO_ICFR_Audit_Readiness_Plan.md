## 0. Read this first — what NASDAQ listing actually requires of you

You are an **EGC under the JOBS Act**, which changes *when* certain things are required, not *whether* your controls must be real:

- **Deferred:** SOX **404(b)** *external-auditor attestation* on ICFR — up to 5 fiscal years post-IPO (or until you lose EGC status: >$1.235B revenue, large-accelerated-filer status, or >$1B non-convertible debt over 3 years).
- **Still required from your first/second annual report:** SOX **302** CEO/CFO certifications and SOX **404(a)** *management's* assessment of ICFR.
- **Always tested:** Your **independent financial-statement auditor** will still test the IT-dependent and key controls they rely on for the financial audit. That is the "auditor + IT auditor" visit you are preparing for.

**Practical conclusion:** build the controls now and evidence that they *operate*. The deferral lowers the formal-attestation bar, **not** the bar for sound, documented, operating internal control.

## 5. How the auditor will test — and what we produce

For each key control the auditor performs **Test of Design (ToD)** and **Test of Operating Effectiveness (ToE)**:

1. **Walkthrough** — one transaction end to end (e.g., a sales order → invoice → AR receipt → GL posting), confirming the control is designed to prevent/detect the risk.
2. **Sample testing** — a sample of occurrences over the period, verifying the control operated each time (e.g., 25 manual JEs all show a distinct approver).

**Population & sampling note:** ToE needs a period of operation. Controls implemented late in the roadmap give the auditor a *shorter* operating window and a smaller reliable sample — another reason to land Phase 1/2 controls early.
