## 2. Purpose

To define and control the fixed-asset lifecycle ‚Äî acquisition/capitalization, the asset register, periodic depreciation, custody/location tracking, and disposal ‚Äî so that property, plant and equipment and accumulated depreciation are **valid, complete, accurate, properly cut off, and authorized**, that net book value (NBV) is fairly stated, and that every depreciation and disposal posting reaches the GL as a balanced journal entry.

## 3. Scope

**In scope:** asset categories and capitalization defaults (`/api/assets/categories`), asset acquisition (`POST /api/assets`, FA-), **capitalization of capital purchases from a goods receipt** (`GET /api/assets/registrations/eligible`, `POST /api/assets/registrations` FAR-, `‚Ä¶/approve|reject`, FA-10), the asset/NBV register (`GET /api/assets`), QR labelling and the **custody-change maker-checker** (`POST /api/assets/scan-update` ‚Üí request; `/api/assets/custody/:reqNo/approve|reject`, FA-11, non-GL) plus **physical audit-by-scan** (`/api/assets/audits*`), the per-asset depreciation schedule (`GET /api/assets/:assetNo/schedule`), the monthly straight-line depreciation run (`POST /api/assets/depreciation/run`, DEP-), and disposal (`PATCH /api/assets/:assetNo/dispose`, DISP-).

**Also in scope ‚Äî Enterprise Asset Management (EAM):** maintenance **work orders** (`/api/eam/work-orders`, corrective/preventive/inspection), **preventive-maintenance (PM) schedules** (`/api/eam/pm-schedules`, time- or meter-based) with an idempotent **due-generation sweep** (`/api/eam/pm/run`, also a daily scheduled job `eam_pm_generate`), and **meter readings** (`/api/eam/assets/:assetNo/meter`). Completing a work order with a vendor cost raises an **AP payable** for the maintenance spend.

**Out of scope:** the manual journal lifecycle and period close that the FA postings flow through (see `04-general-ledger-close.md`), procurement and AP settlement of the asset purchase / maintenance (see `02-procure-to-pay.md`), and VAT on acquisition/disposal (see `06-tax-compliance.md`).

## 6. Roles & responsibilities (RACI)

Single-duty roles enforce SoD: the role that **initiates** an asset acquisition or disposal is never the role that **approves** it (rule **R07**); the role that **posts** the depreciation JE (`gl_post`) is separated from the role that **closes the period** (`gl_close`) (**R05**); and asset-master/category configuration access is administered separately (**R01**).

## 7. Process narrative

1. **Category & capitalization defaults.** FaAccountant maintains asset categories via `POST /api/assets/categories` / `GET /api/assets/categories`; each category carries capitalization defaults (`useful_life_years = 5`, asset acct **1500**, accumulated acct **1590**, depreciation-expense acct **5200**). Category/master access is segregated from posting (**R01**, **FA-04**).
2. **Asset acquisition / capitalization (decision point).** FaAccountant initiates `POST /api/assets`. Useful life is mandatory ‚Äî a request missing `useful_life_months` is rejected `NO_LIFE` (`400`). On capitalization a balanced acquisition JE (doc prefix **FA-**) posts **Dr 1500 Cr 1000** for a cash purchase or **Dr 1500 Cr 2000** for an on-account (AP) purchase; Œ£debit = Œ£credit by construction (**FA-01**, **GL-01**). Capex authorization is segregated from initiation (**R07**).
3. **Asset register / NBV.** `GET /api/assets` returns the register with cost, accumulated depreciation, and NBV totals. The register is the system of record for NBV completeness, reviewed by FinancialController against the GL 1500/1590 balances (**FA-05**, **REC-01**).
4. **QR labelling & custody (non-GL).** `GET /api/assets/:assetNo/qr` and `GET /api/assets/qr/labels` produce asset tags. Each tag encodes the universal payload `ASSET_ID:<no>|DESC:..|LOC:..`; when `WEB_BASE_URL` is configured the printed QR *image* instead encodes a deep link `<base>/q?d=<payload>` so a technician‚Äôs **phone camera opens the resolver page** (`/q`) that identifies the asset and links into the register. Custody capture works three ways off the same tag: the **in-app camera scanner** (native `BarcodeDetector` where present, else a lazy-loaded JS decoder so it works on **any browser with a camera** ‚Äî iPhone/Safari, Firefox, Android; reads QR **and** 1D barcodes), a **hardware wedge scanner**, or manual entry ‚Äî all feed `POST /api/assets/scan-update` (which tolerates either carrier ‚Äî raw payload or the `/q?d=` URL). **Custody-change maker-checker (FA-11, non-GL):** confirming the current location/holder logs a `Scan Verify` movement immediately (existence evidence); any **change** to location/holder does **not** touch the register ‚Äî it raises a **PendingApproval custody-change request** (`asset_scan_requests`), and a **different** user must approve via `POST /api/assets/custody/:reqNo/approve` before the register moves and a `Scan Update` movement is written. Self-approve ‚Üí `SOD_VIOLATION` (binds even Admin); `‚Ä¶/reject` leaves the asset in place; one request pending per asset. So a custodian can never silently reassign an asset to themselves ‚Äî the move is segregated from its approval (**FA-11**, existence/custody **FA-04**).

   **Physical audit-by-scan.** `POST /api/assets/audits` opens a count for a location (snapshotting the **expected** register assets); scanning tags (`‚Ä¶/audits/:no/scan`, offline-replay-safe on `client_uuid`) classifies each as **Found** (at the audited location), **Misplaced** (register says elsewhere) or **Unknown** (not in the register); `GET ‚Ä¶/audits/:no` reconciles and adds **Missing** (expected but not scanned). Closing (`‚Ä¶/close`) raises FA-11 custody-change requests for the misplaced assets (proposing the move to where they were found) ‚Äî routed through the same maker-checker. This is the operative existence/completeness procedure for **FA-04**.

   **Reporting surfaces (BI).** Two read-only, schedulable BI report types surface the above: **`asset_audit`** (`GET /api/assets/audit-report`) lists recent audits with their found/missing/misplaced/unknown tallies plus the outstanding custody-change queue; **`asset_verification_exceptions`** (`GET /api/assets/unverified?days=N`) lists every **active asset not physically verified within N days** (default 90) ‚Äî 'verified' being a `Scan Verify`/`Scan Update` movement, with a never-verified asset falling back to its acquisition date. Scheduled **monthly** (via the BI report scheduler, `POST /api/bi/subscriptions`), the exception report is a **detective existence control** (**FA-12**) that drives a recurring count of stale/never-confirmed assets before period-end; it posts nothing (read-only over `fixed_assets` + `asset_movements`).
5. **Depreciation schedule.** `GET /api/assets/:assetNo/schedule` exposes the per-asset straight-line schedule: monthly charge = (cost ‚àí salvage) / `useful_life_months`, with each period‚Äôs charge **capped at NBV ‚àí salvage** so NBV never falls below salvage.
6. **Monthly depreciation run (decision point).** FaAccountant runs `POST /api/assets/depreciation/run` for a period (`YYYY-MM`). The run is **idempotent per `${tenant}:${period}`** ‚Äî a re-run for the same tenant/period does not double-post. Per-tenant it produces **ONE balanced entry per tenant per period** (doc prefix **DEP-**) so each shop trial balance ties: **Dr 5200 Cr 1590**. Fully-depreciated assets flip status when NBV ‚â§ salvage and stop accruing (**FA-02**, **GL-01**). The DEP- entry flows through the normal ledger period guard, so a **closed period is rejected** (`PERIOD_CLOSED`) ‚Äî see `04-general-ledger-close.md`.
7. **Depreciation run review.** `GET /api/assets/depreciation/runs` lists prior runs. FinancialController reviews each run for idempotency (no duplicate period) and balanced posting before close (**FA-02**, **R05**).
8. **Disposal (decision point ‚Äî system-enforced maker-checker).** A disposal is **requested** via `PATCH /api/assets/:assetNo/dispose`. A disposal of an already-disposed asset ‚Üí `ALREADY_DISPOSED` (`400`); one already pending ‚Üí `DISPOSAL_PENDING`; an unknown asset ‚Üí `NOT_FOUND` (`404`). The balanced disposal JE (doc prefix **DISP-**) clears the asset: **Dr 1590** (remove accumulated) + **Dr 1000** (cash proceeds) + **Cr 1500** (remove cost). Gain/loss = proceeds ‚àí NBV: a **gain** posts **Cr 1510**, a **loss** posts **Dr 1510** (**FA-03**, **GL-01**). **Maker-checker (FA-09):** the request posts that JE as a **Draft** (excluded from balances) and flags the asset **`disposal_pending`** ‚Äî the asset is **not** yet disposed and the register does **not** move; a disposal-pending asset is also **frozen from depreciation**. A **different** user must approve via `POST /api/assets/:assetNo/dispose/approve` before it is effective ‚Äî only then does the JE post, the status become **disposed**, and any **revaluation surplus** recycle to retained earnings (**Dr 3200 / Cr 3100**, posted fresh on approval). A self-approve is rejected `SOD_VIOLATION` (binds **even Admin**, reusing the GL-05 ledger approval); `POST ‚Ä¶/dispose/reject` voids the Draft and the asset stays in service. So one person can never write an asset off the books and pocket the proceeds ‚Äî disposal authorization is segregated from custody and from the FA register owner (**FA-09**, **R07**).
9. **Maintenance (EAM).** A maintenance **work order** is raised against a registered asset (`POST /api/eam/work-orders`, **MWO-**; an unknown asset ‚Üí `ASSET_NOT_FOUND`) and progresses through a guarded lifecycle **open ‚Üí in_progress ‚Üí completed/cancelled** (an out-of-order move ‚Üí `BAD_TRANSITION`). On **completion with a vendor and an actual cost**, the maintenance spend is routed through **AP** (`createApTxn`, expense account **5710**): **Dr 5710** net **+ Dr 2100** input VAT **/ Cr 2000** gross ‚Äî so the cost is a payable that settles through the normal AP flow and reconciles (in-house work with no vendor records the cost only). **PM schedules** (`POST /api/eam/pm-schedules`) define a preventive cadence (time `interval_days` and/or `meter_interval`, against `asset_meters` readings); the **due-generation sweep** (`POST /api/eam/pm/run`, cron-callable, and the daily scheduled job **`eam_pm_generate`** via the report scheduler) raises a preventive WO for every due schedule and rolls it forward. The sweep is **idempotent** ‚Äî a schedule with an outstanding generated WO is skipped and its due date is advanced on generation (**FA-06**). A work order captures **labor and parts cost lines** (`POST /api/eam/work-orders/:woNo/lines`, `GET .../lines`): a labor line costs **hours √ó unit_cost**, a part line **quantity √ó unit_cost**, and the WO's **actual cost rolls up** from the sum of its lines (used in place of the estimate when the WO completes, so the AP posting reflects the real spend). Per-asset **reliability & cost KPIs** (`GET /api/eam/assets/:assetNo/reliability`) report corrective failures, preventive count, open WOs, total downtime hours, **MTBF** (mean time between corrective failures), and total maintenance spend ‚Äî the inputs to maintenance budgeting and asset-replacement decisions (**FA-06**).
10. **Revaluation / impairment (decision point).** An asset's carrying amount is adjusted to a new value via `POST /api/assets/:assetNo/revalue` (doc prefix **REVAL-**). An **upward revaluation** (delta > 0) credits the **revaluation surplus in equity**: **Dr 1500 / Cr 3200**. A **downward revaluation (impairment)** (delta < 0) debits **impairment loss**: **Dr 5820 / Cr 1500**. The **gross 1500 moves by the delta** so the register stays tied to the GL (accumulated depreciation unchanged); the asset's `net_book_value` is set to the new value. A no-op revaluation (new value = current NBV) ‚Üí `NO_CHANGE`; a disposed asset ‚Üí `ALREADY_DISPOSED`. Every event is written to the `asset_revaluations` audit trail (`GET /api/assets/:assetNo/revaluations`). On **disposal** of a previously-revalued asset, any **revaluation surplus** held for it in equity is **recycled directly to retained earnings** (**Dr 3200 / Cr 3100**) ‚Äî it does **not** pass through P&L ‚Äî and the recycled amount is reported on the disposal response (**FA-07**, **GL-01**). **Maker-checker (FA-08, system-enforced):** because a fair-value adjustment moves equity/P&L on a subjective input, a revalue/impairment request posts its GL entry as a **Draft** (excluded from balances), sets the `asset_revaluations` record to **`PendingApproval`**, and **does not move the carrying value**. A **different** user must approve via `POST /api/assets/:assetNo/revalue/approve` before it becomes effective and the register is re-valued ‚Äî a self-approve is rejected `SOD_VIOLATION` (binds **even Admin**), reusing the GL-05 ledger approval and re-checking the period is open. `POST ‚Ä¶/revalue/reject` voids the Draft (status `Rejected`). **Only one** revaluation may be pending per asset, and the disposal surplus recycle counts only **approved** revaluations (**FA-08**, **R07**).

11. **Capitalization from a goods receipt (decision point ‚Äî system-enforced maker-checker, FA-10).** Capital purchases enter the register via the procurement chain rather than a manual `POST /api/assets`. An item flagged `is_fixed_asset` on the item master ‚Äî or a PO line flagged `is_capital` for a one-off ‚Äî is, when received, **excluded from inventory capitalization (Dr 1200)** at goods receipt and instead recorded on the GR line as `is_capital` (**eligible**). A preparer lists the eligible lines (`GET /api/assets/registrations/eligible?gr_no=`) and **requests** capitalization (`POST /api/assets/registrations`, doc prefix **FAR-**), supplying the depreciation life (or a category that carries one); the cost defaults to the GR line value. The request posts **nothing** to the GL (`status='PendingApproval'`). A **different** user must approve via `POST /api/assets/registrations/:regNo/approve` before the **fixed_assets** row is created and the acquisition JE posts **effective**: **Dr 1500 / Cr 2000** (received on account). A self-approve is rejected `SOD_VIOLATION` (binds **even Admin**); `‚Ä¶/reject` records the rejection and the line becomes eligible to re-raise. The created asset carries **`source_gr_no` / `source_po_no`** for end-to-end **PR ‚Üí PO ‚Üí GR ‚Üí FA** traceability, and a GR line cannot be capitalized twice (`ALREADY_REGISTERED`). So receiving goods and deciding what enters the asset register (and at what cost/life) are segregated duties (**FA-10**, **R07**, **R04**).

12. **Parallel TAX depreciation book (FIN-6a).** Every acquisition path accepts optional tax-book parameters ‚Äî `tax_useful_life_months` (the Thai tax cap, typically shorter than book), `tax_salvage_value` and `tax_initial_allowance_pct` (the Thai first-year special/initial allowance). When any is supplied the asset keeps a **memo-only parallel tax basis** (`fixed_assets.tax_accumulated_depreciation` / `tax_net_book_value`) alongside the GAAP book. `POST /api/assets/tax-depreciation/run` (period `YYYY-MM`) advances it ‚Äî straight-line over the tax life down to the tax salvage, plus the initial allowance (percent of the depreciable base) in the **first** period ‚Äî and posts **NO GL** (tax depreciation is not a bookkeeping entry). It is idempotent per period (`tax_last_depreciated_period`). Because Thai tax depreciates faster than book, tax NBV < book NBV: a **taxable temporary difference** that flows straight into the **deferred-tax** module (`POST /api/ledger/deferred-tax/run`) ‚Äî which now reads each asset's **actual tax NBV** (falling back to the documented factor approximation only for assets with no tax book) ‚Äî so the deferred-tax charge (Dr 5950 / Cr 1700 or the reverse) is computed from the real book-vs-tax difference instead of a manual GAAP adjustment (**FIN-6a**, **TAX-06**, **GL-01**).

13. **CIP / AUC ‚Äî construction-in-progress capitalization (decision point ‚Äî system-enforced maker-checker, FA-13).** An asset built over time is opened as a **construction-in-progress** record (`POST /api/assets/cip`, doc prefix **CIP-**). Cost lines (from a goods receipt, a project or manual entry) are accumulated onto it via `POST /api/assets/cip/:cipNo/cost`, each posting **Dr 1520 CIP / Cr 2000 AP** (or **Cr 1000 Cash**) and rolling up `accumulated_cost` ‚Äî the CIP is an asset-under-construction and is **NOT depreciated**. Only an **Open** CIP accepts cost (else `CIP_NOT_OPEN`). When complete, capitalization is a **settlement REQUEST** (`POST /api/assets/cip/:cipNo/settle`) carrying the depreciation life (or a category) and a **mandatory reason**; a CIP with no accumulated cost cannot be settled (`CIP_NO_COST`). The request posts **nothing** (`status='PendingSettlement'`). A **different** user must approve via `POST /api/assets/cip/:cipNo/settle/approve` before the **fixed_assets** row is created and the **reclassification JE posts effective: Dr 1500 / Cr 1520** (moving the accumulated construction cost out of CIP into the productive asset, at which point depreciation begins). A self-approve is rejected `SOD_VIOLATION` (binds **even Admin**); `‚Ä¶/settle/reject` re-opens the CIP. The created asset carries **`source_cip_no`** for traceability and (if tax params were supplied) its own tax book. So accumulating construction cost and deciding when/at-what-value it is capitalized are segregated duties (**FA-13**, **R07**).

```mermaid
flowchart TD
    GR0[Capital GR line is_capital - excluded from inventory FA-10] --> GR1[Preparer POST /api/assets/registrations FAR- PendingApproval no GL]

    B -- "Yes" --> C[Post FA- Dr 1500 Cr 1000 or Cr 2000 GL-01]
    C --> D[Asset register NBV GET /api/assets FA-05]
    D --> E[QR label + custody scan-update non-GL FA-04]
    E --> F[Run depreciation POST depreciation/run period YYYY-MM]
    F --> G{Idempotent per tenant:period? FA-02}
    G -- "already run" --> G1[No double-post - skip]
    G -- "new" --> H{Period open? GL period guard}
    H -- "Closed" --> H1[Reject PERIOD_CLOSED]
    H -- "Open" --> I[Post one DEP- per tenant Dr 5200 Cr 1590 GL-01]
    I --> J{NBV <= salvage?}
    J -- "Yes" --> J1[Flip status fully depreciated]
    J -- "No" --> K[Disposal requested]
    J1 --> K
    K --> L{Asset exists and not disposed? FA-03}
    L -- "missing" --> L1[Reject NOT_FOUND 404]
    L -- "already disposed" --> L2[Reject ALREADY_DISPOSED 400]
    L -- "OK approved" --> M[Post DISP- Dr 1590 Dr 1000 Cr 1500 gain Cr 1510 loss Dr 1510 GL-01]
```

**Swimlane description by role:** **FaAccountant** maintains categories, initiates acquisition, and runs monthly depreciation. **CustodianStaff** performs the QR scan-update for location/holder custody (non-GL). The **system** enforces the `NO_LIFE` guard, the per-tenant balanced posting, idempotency per tenant/period, the salvage cap, the fully-depreciated status flip, and routes every FA-/DEP-/DISP- entry through the ledger period guard. **FinancialController** approves capex and disposals, reviews each depreciation run and the NBV register tie-out. **Controller/CFO** owns capitalization defaults and final disposal authority.

## 10. Inputs & outputs

**Inputs:** asset categories + capitalization defaults, acquisition request (cost, salvage, useful life, cash/AP), custody scan events, depreciation period (`YYYY-MM`), disposal request (proceeds).
**Outputs:** capitalized asset + acquisition JE (FA-), asset/NBV register, QR labels, `assetMovements` custody trail, per-tenant monthly depreciation JE (DEP-), disposal JE with gain/loss (DISP-).

## 12. KPIs / metrics

- Acquisitions rejected for `NO_LIFE` (data-quality signal).
- Depreciation re-run double-posts detected (target: 0; idempotency holds).
- Per-tenant TB tie-out exceptions after each DEP- run (target: 0).
- NBV register-to-GL (1500/1590) reconciliation differences (target: 0).
- Disposals processed with correctly computed gain/loss; `ALREADY_DISPOSED` attempts.

## 13. Exception & error handling

| Error code | Trigger | Handling |
|---|---|---|
| `NO_LIFE` (400) | Acquisition without `useful_life_months` | Originator supplies life; resubmit |
| `ALREADY_DISPOSED` (400) | Dispose an asset already disposed | Verify asset status; no action |
| `NOT_FOUND` (404) | Dispose unknown `assetNo` | Verify asset number |
| `PERIOD_CLOSED` | DEP-/disposal JE into a closed period | Re-open per close policy (authorized) or post to open period |
| `SOD_VIOLATION` / SoD conflict | Conflicting initiate/approve or post/close duties (incl. CIP settlement, FA-13) | AccessAdmin remediates (see `08-itgc.md`) |
| `CIP_NOT_OPEN` (400) | Add cost to (or settle) a CIP that is not Open (already PendingSettlement/Capitalized) | Verify CIP status; open a new CIP or act on the pending settlement |
| `CIP_NO_COST` (400) | Settle a CIP with no accumulated cost | Accumulate cost lines before requesting settlement |
