AI is ceiling + metered-overage — never unlimited. Included daily tokens are free; usage between "included" and "max" is billed per 1,000; the first request past the hard ceiling returns AI_BUDGET_EXCEEDED. This ties the price to upstream token COGS so a heavy tenant is never served at negative margin.
No self-serve add-on checkout (by design). Add-ons apply via a per-tenant features.suites override on a custom plan row / invoice line — add-on buyers need implementation anyway. Published prices are anchors, not a transactable cart.

4 · Things you should know

SME is a special edition, not just a cheap tier. It carries the full operational spine (finance→procurement→planning→CRM→AI) at ฿690, fenced by a 1-seat / 1-location cap. control_profile='sme' also relaxes maker-checker (self-approval) — orthogonal to the plan. Upgrading to Enterprise re-instates full SoD + adds the verticals.
14-day self-service trial on a real tenant — data created in trial carries into the paid plan. Trials are entitlement-granted all suites during the window.
Annual = 2 months free (10-month pricing). Multi-currency published (THB + USD); currency resolution is fail-closed — an unlisted currency errors rather than silently falling back to THB.
The "Business" rung was added deliberately to close the old Standard→Professional cliff (2,900→9,900 with nothing between). Steps are now ~1.7× each. Existing subscriptions keep their price until renewal.
Enforcement is behind a kill-switch. Suite/plan gating only bites when ENTITLEMENTS_ENFORCE is on (god-bypassed, trial-granted). Confirm it is enabled in an environment before relying on these fences commercially.
The map is CI-guarded. tools/ci/check-entitlements.mjs fails any PR where a module token is unassigned or the map drifts — every one of the 42 tokens must live in exactly one suite.

5 · Assessment & the underlying idea

The idea in one line — sell bundled suites (not single modules) along two independent axes: breadth (how many suites) and scale (seats / locations / usage quotas). Tiers move both, every tier is a clean superset of the one below, so an upgrade is an entitlement flip, never a migration — the "land & expand" engine. Heavy verticals and variable-cost usage (AI) are held out and priced separately so the ladder stays simple and margin-safe.
Well-judged — the Business rung (procurement + multi-branch) genuinely fixes the price cliff and matches a growing chain's next need. SME is a smart wedge: full ERP for a solo owner, seat-capped so it can't cannibalise the multi-seat ladder. Enterprise correctly bundles the four heavy verticals for the group / pre-IPO buyer. Metering AI on a ceiling model is the right call for COGS discipline.
Resolved (docs/53 Q1) — procurement placement. Base procurement (PR→PO→blind-count GRN) now ships from Standard (฿2,900) up; the premium routing (RFQ, three-way match) remains the ฿1,500 scm_advanced add-on. The receiving-controls story is sellable at the entry bundle.
Resolved (docs/53 Q2 + C1) — bimodality. The ฿690 plan now presents as Solo, the one-seat edition of the Complete line, and the split-sell lines (POS per branch / ERP flat) give feature-first buyers a coherent axis; procurement now also sits in Standard, closing the largest gap. AI remains a Solo/Professional+ differentiator by design.
Watch #3 — Free is a funnel, not a product. No finance, no sales/POS — you cannot run a business on it. That's intentional (evaluation / lead capture), but don't position it as a usable free tier.
Net — post-docs/53 the catalog fights in both market bands on their own axes: per-branch POS SKUs at the floor, flat ERP/bundles against per-user suites at the ceiling, with grandfathered pricing (0454) protecting the installed base through any future repricing.
